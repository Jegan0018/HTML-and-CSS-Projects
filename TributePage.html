Lionel Messi

The Football Maestro who Enthralled A Billion Hearts

Here's a time line of Lionel Messi's Career:

  • 1987 - Born on June 24 in Rosario, Argentina
  • 1995 - Joins the youth team of his local club, Newell's Old Boys, showcasing his football talent from a young age.
  • 2000 - Diagnosed with growth hormone deficiency at the age of 13. Moves to Spain with his family to join FC Barcelona's youth academy, La Masia, after they offered to pay for his medical treatment.
  • 2004 - Makes his debut for FC Barcelona's first team at the age of 17. Becomes the youngest player to score a goal for the club.
  • 2005 - Becomes a regular starter for FC Barcelona and helps the team win the La Liga title.
  • 2006 - Wins the UEFA Champions League with FC Barcelona, scoring a crucial goal in the final against Arsenal.
  • 2009 - Wins the FIFA Ballon d'Or for the first time, marking the beginning of a series of Ballon d'Or victories.
  • 2010 - Helps FC Barcelona win the UEFA Champions League and the FIFA Club World Cup.
  • 2011 - Wins his third consecutive Ballon d'Or, becoming the first player to achieve this feat.
  • 2012 - Breaks Gerd Muller's record for the most goals scored in a calendar year, setting a new milestone with 91 goals.
  • 2014 - Captains Argentina national team in the FIFA World Cup, leading them to the final but ultimately finishing as runners-up.
  • 2015 - Wins the UEFA Champions League and the FIFA Club World Cup with FC Barcelona, also securing his fifth Ballon d'Or.
  • 2016 - Announces his retirement from international football after Argentina's defeat in the Copa America Centenario final.
  • 2017 - Reverses his decision and returns to the Argentina national team.
  • 2019 - Wins the FIFA Best Men's Player award, recognizing his outstanding contributions to football.
